01 — About

Hi, I'm Mingfei. 👋

I'm a PhD student at the University of Technology Sydney, working on Multi-Agent Systems and Agent Memory for large language models. I'm broadly fascinated by the question: how should agents remember, reason, and coordinate so that science and people benefit?

Before UTS, I graduated from China University of Political Science and Law (CUPL). Along the way I've also worked on the Science of Science — modeling how new ideas grow into impact through citation networks, knowledge diffusion, and bias-aware learning. My recent paper From Newborn to Impact (The Web Conference 2026) sits exactly at that intersection.

Always happy to chat about agent memory architectures, multi-agent vs. single-agent baselines, graph-based scientific impact, and anything in between. Reach out →

03 — Research

📝 Selected Publications

WWW 2026 first author

From Newborn to Impact: Bias-Aware Citation Prediction

Mingfei Lu, Mengjia Wu, Jiawei Xu, Weikai Li, Feng Liu, Ying Ding, Yizhou Sun, Jie Lu, Yi Zhang.

Proceedings of The Web Conference (WWW), 2026.

Citation prediction is critical for newborn papers, where early assessment must be performed without citation signals and under highly long-tailed distributions. We model implicit factors of scientific impact and introduce bias-aware learning to deliver stable predictions on lowly cited papers — moving from coarse proxies to a more faithful signal of impact-in-the-making.
